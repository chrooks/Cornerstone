# Lab Consequence Communication & Decision Weight: Research Report (Axis 2 of 2)

*Generated: 2026-07-05 | Sources: 37 | Confidence: High on preview/friction patterns, Medium on roster-tool internals*

> Axis 2 of a two-part research pass for making the Lab build → eval loop feel responsive and game-y.
> This half covers **communicating decision weight and consequence — before commit, at commit, and after**.
> The sibling report ([lab-juice-game-feel.md](./lab-juice-game-feel.md)) covers responsiveness and juice.

## Executive Summary

Decision weight in complex-systems games comes from three moves: preview the resolved consequence before commit (feedforward — research-backed to reduce errors), decompose every aggregate number into named causes on demand (nested attribution), and concentrate friction/ceremony at the single genuinely committing act while keeping exploration frictionless and reversible. Into the Breach, CK3's nested tooltips, and Frostpunk's Book of Laws are the canonical cases for each move; ESPN's Trade Machine and KeepTradeCut are the closest roster-tool analogues. The research caveats are sharp: a bare projected number becomes an anchor users over-trust, dishonest previews get discovered and erode trust in the whole readout (XCOM), and exact deterministic previews risk "solving" the builder into spreadsheet work — fuzzy-band previews (Frostpunk) are the documented alternative.

## 1. Consequence Preview Before Commit

- **Into the Breach is the canonical case for full-consequence telegraphing.** Subset Games removed randomness from enemy attacks entirely and shows every enemy's exact target and damage before the player moves: "We wanted to make something where every death felt like your own fault. This lead us to use of telegraphed enemy attacks as a core mechanic... the game starts to feel like a puzzle" — Justin Ma ([Road to the IGF interview](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/game-platforms/road-to-the-igf-subset-games-i-into-the-breach-i-)). The tradeoff (puzzle feel vs. drama) is discussed in the [GDC 2019 postmortem](https://www.gdcvault.com/play/1025772/-Into-the-Breach-Design) ([slides](https://ubm-twvideo01.s3.amazonaws.com/o1/vault/gdc2019/presentations/Into%20the%20Breach%20Postmortem%20Final.pdf)). Key mechanism: hovering any planned move shows the *complete resolved outcome* before commit — preview of final state, not of inputs.
- **Civilization VII's post-launch patches are a live case study in adding consequence preview after players demanded it.** The Sept 2025 patch made Building Placement and Growth Events "highlight net yield changes, supported by a detailed view offering a full breakdown," and "yield losses from replacing or overbuilding are now previewed on the map" ([Civ VII Patch Notes](https://support.civilization.com/hc/en-us/articles/44950826478483-Civilization-VII-Patch-Notes-September-30-2025); [PC Gamer](https://www.pcgamer.com/games/strategy/civilization-7-patch-1-0-1-addresses-key-areas-of-player-reported-issues-including-ui-and-tooltip-improvements/)). The community shipped a *Policy Yields Preview* mod ([CivFanatics](https://forums.civfanatics.com/threads/list-civ-7-mods-that-make-the-game-and-ui-more-understandable-especially-for-a-new-player.696672/)) — "show me the delta before I commit" is a felt player need, not designer indulgence.
- **Slay the Spire's Intent system** previews the opponent's next action, adjusted live by current modifiers — playing a card that applies Weak visibly lowers the previewed incoming damage before you end the turn ([StS Wiki — Intent](https://slaythespire.wiki.gg/wiki/Intent)). The StS2 mod scene shows the failure mode: icon-only previews without numbers force memorization; the best mods "aren't making the game easier — they're making it clearer" ([Medium](https://medium.com/@willan2028lima/the-best-slay-the-spire-2-mods-arent-making-the-game-easier-they-re-making-it-clearer-e0db0c57882b), single-source).
- **XCOM's shot-percentage breakdown is preview with a trust caveat.** The pre-commit panel itemizes hit-chance contributions (cover, height, flanking) ([UFOpaedia](https://www.ufopaedia.org/index.php/Chance_to_Hit_(EU2012))), but the displayed number is silently massaged by hidden aim-assist on lower difficulties; community mods exist purely to surface the real modifiers ([Enhanced Hit Chance Displays](https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=624406557)). A preview that lies — even benevolently — gets discovered and erodes trust in the whole readout.
- **HCI framing: this family is "feedforward"** — information given *before* the action about what its result will be, versus feedback after ([ACM TOCHI](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3603623); framework origins in [Interaction Frogger](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/221441504_Interaction_frogger_A_design_framework_to_couple_action_and_function_through_feedback_and_feedforward)). Feedforward measurably reduces errors (§5).

## 2. Visualizing Cascading Effects

- **Paradox's nested tooltips (CK3) are the reference pattern for drill-down attribution.** Any highlighted concept inside a tooltip opens a deeper tooltip, recursively — progressive disclosure of the causal chain on demand ([CK3 Dev Diary #16](https://forum.paradoxplaza.com/forum/threads/ck3-dev-diary-16-tutorials-and-tooltips-and-encyclopedias-oh-my.1345581/); analysis in [philip.design](https://philip.design/blog/tooltips-in-tooltips/)). Two extracted principles: reveal only when requested; let depth match the user's curiosity. Downsides: cursor-precision fragility, unclear mobile mapping, and a vocal minority who find nesting annoying ([Paradox forum dissent](https://forum.paradoxplaza.com/forum/threads/reminder-to-the-devs-that-nested-tooltips-is-bad-ux-design.1702017/)). Stellaris does the same additive attribution — hovering shows the itemized bonus breakdown by source ([forum thread](https://forum.paradoxplaza.com/forum/threads/can-we-get-detailed-tooltips-on-console-please.1566928/), weak/single-source).
- **Football Manager makes social cascade legible** with a hierarchy pyramid (Team Leaders → Highly Influential → Influential → Other; top-level unhappiness "will not only affect them but also permeate through the rest of the squad") plus a per-player happiness ledger decomposed into named causes (Promises, Morale, Training, Treatment, Club, Management) with an Overall roll-up ([FM24 manual — Dynamics](https://community.sports-interactive.com/sigames-manual/football-manager-2024/your-squad-team-report-and-dynamics-r4957/)). Contribution attribution for a *social* system: aggregate score, decomposed by cause, weighted by network position.
- **FM26's "tile and card" rebuild is the compression pattern**: every screen is tiles (snapshot numbers) that open into cards (full detail) — summary→drilldown at platform scale ([FM26 UI](https://www.footballmanager.com/fm26/features/fm26s-reimagined-user-interface)).
- **NBA 2K surfaces chemistry effects at the moment they fire**: 2K26 added "Teammate Chemistry boosts are now displayed in the HUD when earned" ([2K26 patch notes](https://nba.2k.com/2k26/patch-updates/)) — attribution at the point of consequence rather than buried in a menu. Chemistry model context in [2K community docs](https://nba.2k.com/2k22/up-your-game/myteam-tips/myteam-basics/).
- **Factory sims externalize ripple effects as time-series.** Factorio graphs production/consumption per item; its documented legibility failure — high-volume items compress everything else's scale, fixed by per-item filtering — is a direct warning for any "everything on one chart" aggregate view ([Factorio Wiki](https://wiki.factorio.com/Production_statistics); [FFF #408](https://factorio.com/blog/post/fff-408)). RimWorld needed mods for equivalent breakdowns ([More Graphs](https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=1966250412)).
- **Frostpunk previews cascades qualitatively, not numerically** — a law shows "Hope will increase slightly" plus unlocked buildings, deliberately fuzzy to preserve risk tension ([Gabriel Chauri](https://www.gabrielchauri.com/frostpunk-decisions/)). Fuzzy deltas are a legitimate alternative to exact deltas when you *want* residual uncertainty.

## 3. Deliberate Friction and Ceremony

- **Frostpunk's Book of Laws is the canonical commitment device.** The game pauses when the book opens (time to deliberate); signing is animated as ceremony; laws are irreversible; a cooldown blocks the next law ([Gabriel Chauri](https://www.gabrielchauri.com/frostpunk-decisions/); [gamepressure](https://guides.gamepressure.com/frostpunk/guide.asp?ID=41370)). Extracted principle: "profound decisions" (>10s deliberation, moral weight) earn ceremony; the weight comes from mechanical permanence + previewed-but-fuzzy consequence + moral framing combined.
- **Fire Emblem's permadeath shows friction's double edge.** Permanent death gives every decision weight — and most players *reset the map* rather than accept the loss ([Strategy Lair](https://www.strategylair.com/articles/fire-emblem-engages-fell-xenologue-dlc-abandons-permadeath-fueling-longtime-fan-concerns.html); [CritPoints](https://critpoints.net/2016/10/08/forcing-permadeath-in-tactics-games/)). The series' own trajectory (Casual mode, Divine Pulse rewind) is a designer admission that pure irreversibility overshoots. Lesson: irreversibility creates weight but also save-scumming; bounded rewind keeps the weight without the reset loop.
- **NN/g on confirmations**: use only for serious, hard-to-undo consequences; the dialog must restate the *specific* action and its effects (never bare "Are you sure?"); button labels name the action; overuse habituates users into auto-dismissal; undo is generally the superior safeguard ([NN/g — Confirmation Dialogs](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/confirmation-dialog/)). For truly dangerous ops, require a nonstandard act (typing a word) — effortful confirmation as manufactured ceremony.
- **The counterweight is Shneiderman's Golden Rule 6**: "actions should be reversible... relieves anxiety... and encourages exploration of unfamiliar options" ([Eight Golden Rules](https://www.cs.umd.edu/users/ben/goldenrules.html)). Synthesis: keep *exploration* (add/remove/swap while drafting) reversible and frictionless; concentrate friction at the one genuinely committing act (publish/save/submit team).
- **Scarcity cues as ambient weight**: DraftKings-style builders keep the salary cap permanently visible — total used, remaining, and *average remaining per open slot* — updating live with every add/remove ([Topend Sports](https://www.topendsports.com/en-us/sports-betting/dfs/draftkings-dfs/), single-source for the per-slot detail). Per-open-slot average is the strongest scarcity cue found: it converts remaining budget into "what quality of player can I still afford," making each pick's opportunity cost felt.

## 4. Roster-Builder-Specific Prior Art

- **ESPN NBA Trade Machine — team-level delta from one transaction.** After validating cap legality, it shows each team's *projected record change* ("this trade makes the Lakers +4 wins"), computed from PER of the post-trade rotation ([ESPN Trade Machine](https://www.espn.com/nba/trademachine); mechanism in [Bleacher Report](https://bleacherreport.com/articles/345919)). Closest existing analogue to Cornerstone's problem: single roster change → single legible team-level number, plus a hard rules-gate that fails loudly when cap-illegal. Modern equivalents ([Fanspo](https://fanspo.com/nba/trade-machine), [Spotrac](https://www.spotrac.com/nba/trade-machine)) add richer cap detail.
- **KeepTradeCut — crowdsourced values plus an explicit "Value Adjustment."** Each trade side sums player values, but KTC shows a visible adjustment term that changes as you add/remove players (consolidation premium: one stud > sum of parts), and the balance meter tilts live ([KTC](https://keeptradecut.com/trade-calculator); mechanics reverse-engineered at [Javelin FF](https://www.javelinfantasyfootball.com/2022/09/30/how-the-ktc-adjustment/)). Notable: the nonlinear term is *shown as its own line item* rather than hidden inside the total — attribution of the non-obvious part of the math.
- **Competing value-source models**: [FantasyCalc](https://www.fantasycalc.com/database) derives values from real league trades; [Dynasty Daddy](https://dynasty-daddy.com/trade-calculator) solves from completed trades with smoothing to "avoid wild day-to-day swings" — a deliberate anti-jitter choice so values feel stable enough to trust.
- **Football Manager transfers**: FM communicates transfer consequence mostly through *reaction*, not forecast — dynamics/happiness shifts, scout reports, board/fan responses after the fact ([FM26 UI](https://www.footballmanager.com/fm26/features/fm26s-reimagined-user-interface)). No good source found describing a true pre-commit "team impact preview" in FM (see Gaps).
- **NBA 2K MyTEAM**: lineup screens communicate player quality via card tiers/overalls; chemistry effects historically ran in the background, with 2K26 moving boosts into the HUD ([2K26 patch notes](https://nba.2k.com/2k26/patch-updates/)). Weak prior art for pre-commit deltas; strong prior art for tiered-card hierarchy making roster slots feel valuable.
- **DraftKings/DFS builders**: live cap tracker + remaining-per-slot + instant validity feedback (§3).

## 5. Evidence and Failure Modes

- **Feedforward reduces errors — research-backed.** "Feedforward techniques that allow users to anticipate the consequences of their actions have been shown to significantly decrease errors" ([ACM TOCHI 2023](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3603623); grounding in [Wensveen et al.](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/221441504_Interaction_frogger_A_design_framework_to_couple_action_and_function_through_feedback_and_feedforward)).
- **Choice/information overload is real but conditional.** [Scheibehenne et al.'s meta-analysis](https://scheibehenne.com/ScheibehenneGreifenederTodd2010.pdf) (50 experiments, N≈5,036) found a mean effect near zero; [Chernev et al.](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1057740814000916) (N=7,202) found overload appears specifically under choice-set complexity, task difficulty, preference uncertainty, and time pressure — and experts with strong preferences *benefit* from more options. Implication: dense breakdowns hurt novices under pressure, help invested experts; progressive disclosure serves both.
- **Anchoring on displayed projections — research-backed.** Forecasters anchor on salient displayed numbers and adjust insufficiently ([ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S2214635016300296)); in AI-assisted decisions, overreliance on a displayed confidence number occurs when shown *alone*, mitigated by pairing it with explanations or delaying its display ([anchoring in AI-assisted decisions](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0268401225000076); classic DSS work: [Countering anchoring with DSS](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0167923600000749)). Direct implication: a big projected eval number shown pre-commit *will* anchor users; ship it with its breakdown, never naked.
- **"Players optimize the fun out of a game" — designer-consensus, not experimental.** Soren Johnson documents exploitable optimality in Civ: once learned, it can't be unlearned and turns strategy into tedium ([Water Finds a Crack](https://www.designer-notes.com/game-developer-column-17-water-finds-a-crack/)). Corollary: if the eval preview is exact and the system deterministic, the "best" roster becomes a lookup and building collapses into spreadsheet work. Remedies: rebalancing dominant strategies, randomness, multiple viable builds ([Design for Theorycrafting](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/design-for-theorycrafting)).
- **Theorycrafting as a feature, not a bug — designer-consensus.** Systems should be honest and interactive enough that community analysis *is* the endgame; non-linearity protects exploration ([Design for Theorycrafting](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/design-for-theorycrafting)). A significant player minority treats system mastery as the core fun; obscuring numbers just moves the work to wikis ([MU Soapbox](https://musoapbox.net/topic/2117/game-design-avoiding-min-maxing), forum-grade).
- **Interesting decisions need understood scope — canon.** Sid Meier (GDC 2012): interesting decisions are tradeoffs that are situational, personal, persistent — and players must "understand the full scope of their choices"; a choice everyone makes identically isn't a decision ([Game Developer](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/gdc-2012-sid-meier-on-how-to-see-games-as-sets-of-interesting-decisions)).
- **Dishonest previews get caught.** XCOM's massaged percentages spawned distrust and transparency mods (§1).

## Pattern Catalog for the Lab

1. **Full-consequence telegraph** (Into the Breach). Preview the *resolved end state*, not the input. Mapping: hovering a player card in the pool shows the complete post-add eval — new total, every subscore delta, synergies gained/lost — before the click. [IGF interview](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/game-platforms/road-to-the-igf-subset-games-i-into-the-breach-i-).
2. **Net-delta badge + on-demand full breakdown** (Civ VII patches). Headline the net change; keep the itemized math one interaction deeper. Mapping: "+3.2 Spacing / −1.8 Rim Pressure" chips on hover; click expands the full contribution table. [Civ VII patch notes](https://support.civilization.com/hc/en-us/articles/44950826478483-Civilization-VII-Patch-Notes-September-30-2025).
3. **Nested drill-down attribution** (CK3 tooltips-in-tooltips). Every number explains itself recursively, on demand. Mapping: eval total → subscores → per-player contributions → the specific skill/synergy driving each — each level a hover/tap deeper, never all at once. [philip.design](https://philip.design/blog/tooltips-in-tooltips/).
4. **Live intent adjustment** (Slay the Spire). Previews re-resolve instantly as context changes. Mapping: ghosted eval deltas on every pool card re-compute as the roster changes, so users *see* that Player X's value depends on who's already rostered. [StS Intent wiki](https://slaythespire.wiki.gg/wiki/Intent).
5. **Itemized score panel with honesty constraint** (XCOM + KTC). Decompose the headline number into named causes; never fudge it. Mapping: eval panel lists each subscore's inputs; if the engine has nonlinearities (bell curves), show them as their own labeled line, KTC-style. [UFOpaedia](https://www.ufopaedia.org/index.php/Chance_to_Hit_(EU2012)); [KTC adjustment](https://www.javelinfantasyfootball.com/2022/09/30/how-the-ktc-adjustment/).
6. **Team-delta headline** (ESPN Trade Machine). One transaction → one legible team-level consequence. Mapping: after any swap, lead with a single delta in outcome-flavored units ("eval 72 → 78") before subscores; loud hard-fail when cap rules break. [Bleacher Report](https://bleacherreport.com/articles/345919).
7. **Visible balance meter with named adjustment** (KeepTradeCut). Expose the non-obvious math term as its own line so the tool reads as honest. Mapping: if two 80-rated players don't sum linearly, show "fit adjustment −4" explicitly instead of a mysteriously lower total. [KTC](https://keeptradecut.com/trade-calculator).
8. **Scarcity ambient display** (DraftKings). Remaining budget ÷ remaining slots = felt opportunity cost. Mapping: persistent cap bar with "avg $ per open slot"; watching affordable-player tiers shrink as stars are added makes each add *feel* expensive. [Topend Sports](https://www.topendsports.com/en-us/sports-betting/dfs/draftkings-dfs/).
9. **Influence pyramid / cause-ledger** (FM Dynamics). Aggregate team state decomposed by named cause and weighted by player importance. Mapping: synergy web where the cornerstone's edges render heavier; each subscore backed by a per-player cause list. [FM24 manual](https://community.sports-interactive.com/sigames-manual/football-manager-2024/your-squad-team-report-and-dynamics-r4957/).
10. **Ceremony at the commit point only** (Frostpunk Book of Laws). Pause + restate + ritualize the one irreversible act; leave everything before it fluid. Mapping: add/remove stays instant and undoable; "Save & Publish team" gets a full-screen moment restating final eval, version pin (RuleSet Version + Evaluation Version), and leaderboard consequence. [Gabriel Chauri](https://www.gabrielchauri.com/frostpunk-decisions/).
11. **Consequence-restating confirmation** (NN/g). Confirm rarely; when confirming, name the specific consequence and label buttons with the action. Mapping: "Remove Curry — team loses Elite Spacing (−6.1). [Remove Curry] [Keep Curry]". Never "Are you sure?". [NN/g](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/confirmation-dialog/).
12. **Bounded reversibility** (Fire Emblem's rewind evolution + Shneiderman). Irreversibility creates weight but triggers reset-scumming; bounded undo keeps both weight and flow. Mapping: free undo while drafting; published teams immutable (already enforced by version pinning) — the immutability itself is the weight signal. [CritPoints](https://critpoints.net/2016/10/08/forcing-permadeath-in-tactics-games/); [Shneiderman](https://www.cs.umd.edu/users/ben/goldenrules.html).
13. **Fuzzy-band preview** (Frostpunk "Hope will increase slightly"). Qualitative/banded deltas preserve discovery where exact numbers would solve the game. Mapping: option to show tier-band deltas ("Spacing: B+ → A−") instead of decimals pre-commit, revealing exact numbers only in the post-commit eval report. [Gabriel Chauri](https://www.gabrielchauri.com/frostpunk-decisions/).

## Failure Modes

- **The lying preview.** Any gap between previewed delta and actual post-commit eval (rounding, stale cache, hidden modifiers) reads as dishonesty, XCOM-style; users will diff your numbers ([UFOpaedia](https://www.ufopaedia.org/index.php/Chance_to_Hit_(EU2012))).
- **Naked-number anchoring.** A headline projected score shown alone becomes the anchor for the whole decision ([anchoring research](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0268401225000076)). Always pair with breakdown or delay the roll-up.
- **The solved builder.** Exact, deterministic previews on a fixed pool converge on one optimal roster per RuleSet; exploration dies into lookup ([Water Finds a Crack](https://www.designer-notes.com/game-developer-column-17-water-finds-a-crack/)). Mitigations: fuzzy bands pre-commit, context-dependent values (synergy makes value situational), rotating formats.
- **Confirmation fatigue.** Ceremony on routine actions (every add/remove) trains auto-dismissal and destroys the one confirmation that matters ([NN/g](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/confirmation-dialog/)).
- **Reset-scumming.** Hard irreversibility mid-flow makes users abandon/restart rather than live with outcomes — weight without learning ([CritPoints](https://critpoints.net/2016/10/08/forcing-permadeath-in-tactics-games/)).
- **Aggregate-chart illegibility.** One chart for all subscores lets the loudest metric compress the rest into noise (Factorio's documented graph problem); per-metric filtering or small multiples required ([Factorio Wiki](https://wiki.factorio.com/Production_statistics)).
- **Icon-only previews.** Consequence glyphs without numbers force memorization and get modded into text ([StS2 community response](https://medium.com/@willan2028lima/the-best-slay-the-spire-2-mods-arent-making-the-game-easier-they-re-making-it-clearer-e0db0c57882b)).
- **Overload for novices under pressure.** Dense attribution tables hurt exactly the new-user segment; experts benefit from the same density — one fixed disclosure level fails one group ([Chernev et al.](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1057740814000916)).

## Key Takeaways

- Weight comes from *seeing* consequence: hover-preview the resolved eval delta before commit (feedforward is research-backed to reduce errors).
- Every aggregate number must explain itself on demand — nested attribution, never a naked score (anchoring research demands the breakdown).
- Keep drafting frictionless and undoable; concentrate all ceremony at Save & Publish (Frostpunk pattern).
- Never fudge a preview — one discovered lie poisons the whole readout (XCOM).
- Consider fuzzy tier-band deltas pre-commit to protect exploration from the solved-builder trap; exact numbers post-commit.

## Methodology

Research agent using WebSearch + WebFetch (no firecrawl/exa MCP configured). 2–3 keyword variations per sub-question; ~37 unique sources, deep-reads flagged inline. Sub-questions: (1) consequence preview before commit, (2) cascading-effect visualization, (3) deliberate friction and ceremony, (4) roster-builder prior art, (5) evidence and failure modes.

## Gaps

- **FM pre-commit transfer/tactic forecasting** — no quality source describes FM showing a projected team-impact delta before a transfer; its consequence model is reactive. Don't claim FM "transfer previews."
- **Out of the Park Baseball trade UI** — nothing specific found; unverified beyond genre generalities.
- **Into the Breach postmortem quotes** — vault talk is video/PDF; quotable material comes from the IGF interview + slides listing.
- **Experimental evidence that exact previews reduce exploration** — the "solved-game" risk is entirely designer-consensus (Johnson, Vu, Meier); no controlled study found. Strong face validity, but folklore.
- **Stellaris/EU4 "+X from Y" attribution rationale** — pattern is real and ubiquitous but only weakly sourced; CK3's dev diary is the best-documented Paradox source.
