# Team Building Assessment Heuristics
- Spot-Up Shooting, Movement Shooting, Passing*, Cutting, Offensive Rebounding, PnR Finishing, Vertical Spacing, Screen Setting, Passing*, and Transition Threat*, High Flyer* are all off-ball skills.
- Off-Dribble Shooting, Isolation Scoring, Transition Threat*, Passing*, PnR Ball Handling, Crafty Finishing, Driving, Mid/Low-Post Playing, and High Flying are on-ball skills.
- Passer, Transition Threat, High Flyer are both on and off ball skills.

- Passers, Versatile Defenders, Rim Protectors, Offensive Rebounders, Spot-Up Shooters, and especially Movement Shooters are the highest value skills in terms of roster flexibility.

## Defense
- Good Rim Protectors significantly maximize good Perimeter Disruptors & Versatile Defenders. A good defense usually has at least one good one. (e.g. How Wemby anchoring the Spurs defense empowers their perimeter defenders)
- The impact a good perimeter disruptor by themsef isn't incredibly valuable. Their impact compounds the more perimeter disruptors you have. (e.g. The Thunder having 5 Proficient+ Perimeter Defenders)
- Not having an amazing Rim Protector can be overcame by having a stable of Versatile Defenders (and to a lesser degree Perimeter Disruptors). (e.g. Pistons have a top-3 Defense without a Proficient+ Rim Protector by playing all Capable+ Versa. Def's)
- Versatile Defenders, compound with Perimeter Disruptors just to a lesser degree.
- If you aren't at least a capable defender (Perimeter Disruptor, Versatile Defender, Rim Protector), then you better be Elite at something else. Not being capable in any of these considerably hinders a team's defense. (e.g. Lakers' subpar defense while playing Reaves and Luka)
- Rebounding is needed to end defensive posessions. However good your defense is, it will receive a penalty by not having enough rebounding.
- Rebounding can be done by committee with a bunch of capable-proficient rebounders or by 1 (ideally 2) elite rebounders.
- The defensive impact a smaller player with the same skill tier level < a larger player

## Offense
- Spacing is the single most important aspect of a good offense. You must have enough Off-Ball Shooting to allow your players' on-ball skills to be effective. Not having enough spacing hard caps the impact of those skills.
- Movement Shooters >> Spot Up Shooters in terms of adding spacing.

- Good Transition Threat + Good Passer = Transition Playmakers who maximize other Transition threats. Bonus points  if those recipients are High Flyers
- Good PnR Ball Handlers are best maximiuzed with good PnR Finishers. Bonus points if they're also good vertical spacers, screen setters, or spot up shooters.

- A great on-ball player can both score and pass proficiently and ideally do one or both  of those things at an elite level.
- Good Passers accentuate all other off-ball skills (except Offensive Rebounders). This effect compounds the more good passers there are.
- The value of a good Driver is maximized by his ability to score (Crafty Finishing, High Flyer) and pass.  The more drivers the merrier, as long as the spacing permits.
- Like Drivers, good Low Post players need spacing to work effectively. The best Low-Post Players are good Crafty Finishers, Passers, and/or Offensive Rebounders. 
- Every team needs a reliable means of getting paint touches whether its thru Driving, a Vertical Spacer, or playing in the Post.
- Mid-Post players also need spacing to work, although slighly less than Low-Post Players and Drivers. The best can Pass, Finish (Driver + Craft Finisher or High Flyer), and/or Shoot Off-Dribble.
- Same goes for Iso Scorers, they benefit from spacing albeit slightly less than Mid-Post Players. Te Best ones can Pass, Score in different ways (Off-Dribble, Post, At The Rim).
- Every decent offense needs at least one good on-ball player, ideally two. If there's only one, the better a Passer and Scorer (Iso, Low/Mid Post) they need to be. 
- Exclusively on-ball players need to be Elite at what they do to justify their position. As it's very difficult to build an optimal team with more than one exclusively on-ball player. The worse you are off-ball the better you need to be on-ball.
- Offensive rebounding is incredibly valuable. Having one more more offensive rebounders can help mitigate (albeit not completely alleviate) poor spacing.
